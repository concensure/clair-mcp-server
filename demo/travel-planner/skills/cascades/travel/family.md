# Family Travel Skill

## When to Use

Load when the user mentions family, kids, children, baby, or toddler in their travel plans.

## Guidelines

- **Babies (0–2)**: Cribs, feeding facilities, stroller accessibility, nap schedules.
- **Toddlers (2–5)**: Short attention spans, playgrounds, interactive museums.
- **Kids (5+)**: Age-appropriate activities, interests (animals, science, adventure).
- Pace the itinerary: max 2–3 activities per day, build in flexibility.
- Pack essentials: snacks, entertainment, first-aid, familiar comfort items.

## Accommodation Tips

- Request adjoining rooms or family suites.
- Kitchen/kitchenette helpful for baby food and snacks.
- Check pool hours and safety (fenced, shallow areas).

## Flight Tips

- Pre-board with infants; request bassinet for long-haul.
- Bring snacks, toys, and change of clothes in carry-on.
- Consider flight timing around nap schedules.

## Output Format

Include a "Family Considerations" section with:
- Baby/toddler-friendly amenities
- Suggested pacing
- Emergency/medical info (local hospitals, pharmacies)
